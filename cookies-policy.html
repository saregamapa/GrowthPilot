This Cookie Policy explains how BrokerBoost uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website. It is designed to help you understand what cookies are, why we use them, and how you can control them.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better browsing experience, and give website owners useful information about how their site is being used.

Cookies can be "session cookies" (which expire when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (which remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them). They can also be "first-party" (set by us) or "third-party" (set by external services we use).

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Ensure the website functions correctly and securely
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Analyse how visitors use our website to improve performance
  • Measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns
  • Deliver relevant content and advertisements

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our website are set by third-party services that appear on our pages. We do not control these cookies, and they are governed by the privacy policies of the respective third parties. These include:

  • Google Analytics — for website traffic and behaviour analysis. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Meta (Facebook) Pixel — for tracking conversions and audience retargeting. See Meta's Privacy Policy.
  • LinkedIn Insight Tag — for professional audience analytics and retargeting. See LinkedIn's Privacy Policy.
  • Calendly — for scheduling embedded booking functionality. See Calendly's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Fonts — for loading web fonts. See Google's Privacy Policy.

5. Cookie Duration

Cookies we use have the following retention periods:

  • Session cookies — deleted automatically when you close your browser
  • Short-term persistent cookies — retained for up to 30 days
  • Long-term persistent cookies — retained for up to 2 years (e.g. Google Analytics)

6. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

Browser settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can set your browser to refuse all cookies, to alert you when a cookie is being set, or to delete cookies that have already been set. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website.

For guidance on managing cookies in your specific browser, visit:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Opt-out tools

  • Google Analytics: Install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Interest-based advertising: Visit aboutads.info or youronlinechoices.eu

7. Consent

When you first visit our website, we will ask for your consent to use non-essential cookies via our cookie consent banner. You can choose to accept all cookies, reject non-essential cookies, or customise your preferences. You can change your preferences at any time by clearing your browser cookies and revisiting our site.

Strictly necessary cookies do not require your consent as they are essential for the website to function.
